A Feminised Profession Under Pressure

Structural · Cultural · Policy
StructuralReality
~70% women teachers in compulsory education; 74.48% in primary schools (MoE 2023; ~75% in 2024). Underrepresented in leadership and compensation.
CulturalConstraint
The profession is maternalised — "stability", "long vacations" naturalise the gendered division of labour.
PolicyImpetus
Post-Double Reduction: non-teaching tasks ↑ and role conflicts intensified.

Research Questions

One core question  ·  three sub-questions
How are women primary teachers placed at a bordered position among public teaching, private mothering, and social reproduction — and who bears the institutional cost?
Q1 · Institutional

School–Family Borders

How are responsibility borders moved through collaboration, tutoring, parent communication, and evaluation?

Q2 · Reproductive

Time · Body · Kinship

Once pressure enters the family, how is it redistributed along gendered and intergenerational lines?

Q3 · Affective

Naturalised Emotion

How are patience, empathy, and self-restraint converted into unpaid emotional labour?

Feminist Qualitative Case + Narrative Inquiry

i.

Literature Review

86 documents — teacher-mother scholarship, emotional labour, educational maternalisation, Asian developmentalist–neoliberal governance.

ii.

Semi-Structured Interviews

3 women primary teachers, Jiangsu; 1–2 interviews each lasting 2h.
Daily life, school–family communication, mothering, division of labour, emotional management.

iii.

Participant Observation

Office observation at one focal teacher's school office — school–family communication, fragmented-time use, and teaching / mothering interaction.

iv.

Thematic Analysis

Braun & Clarke (2006). Three recurring themes:

  • moving school–family borders
  • second shift & care chains
  • maternalised emotional labour

04.1 · Institutional Bordering
14 / 25

The School Domain

School–family collaboration expands teacher responsibility beyond teaching.
Case A · Teacher ZhaoA parent came to Zhao about hitting his 6-year-old son for a "bad behaviour"; Zhao suggested a medical check first.
I just want to know that, before hitting him, the parent had at least ruled out some physical or developmental reason.
Teacher Zhao
我只是想知道，你打他之前，你先排除一些生理或者发展上的原因吧。
Case B · Teacher GongThe principal asked her to keep struggling students after class for extra tutoring — unpaid, unscheduled, and never formally required.
Sometimes when children haven't finished homework, or the weaker ones, we had to keep them after school… all of that is basically an obligation.
Teacher Gong
有的时候会有一些孩子的作业没有做好，或者是后进生，我们那时候还要把他留下来……那个都是就相当于义务的。
What the Data Show

The teacher's legitimate scope silently expands into developmental judgement, family-education intervention, and unpaid after-school compensation — legitimised through the discourse of "obligation" and "the child is in our hands". No policy authorises this; a single conversation, a message, or a stay-back is enough.

04.2 · Reproductive Bordering
15 / 25

The Home Domain — Time & Body

Home becomes a site where school responsibility and educational labour continue.
Case A · Teacher Yu · TimeEvenings run non-stop from 17:30 pick-up to past 10pm school work — Yu's own shower is squeezed in only when her child is reading.
When she takes a shower, I make the bed; when she starts bedtime reading, I squeeze in my own shower. After she is fully asleep, I do laundry. After ten, I continue with school work.
Teacher Yu
她洗澡时，我会整理床铺；她开始睡前阅读，我则见缝插针自己洗澡。等到她完全入睡再洗衣服或处理各类家务，十点之后，继续忙学校工作。
Case B · Teacher Zhao · BodyZhao's postpartum body was on the same institutional clock — during a district-level writing assessment, she had no window to pump milk.
I couldn't pump milk in time — I felt like I was about to run a fever.
Teacher Zhao
来不及吸奶，感觉整个人要发高烧。
What the Data Show

Home becomes a continuation of school responsibility. Yu's evening compresses children's learning, housework, and school preparation into the same body; her personal time (bathing, rest) becomes the last residue absorbed by everything else. Zhao's postpartum body — pumping, feeding, recovery — is governed by the school timetable. Time and body are reallocated on institutional terms instead of private ones.

04.2 · Reproductive Bordering
16 / 25

A Hidden Care Infrastructure in Kinship

Pressure travels along the grandmother – mother – child chain.
Case A · The GrandmotherGong's mother, now living with her, took over the three daily meals — freeing Gong for school work by day and companionship at night.
My mother manages breakfast, lunch, and dinner… In the evening I squeeze out time to be with my girl. Yes — that is our division of labour.
Teacher Gong
所有的早上中午，包括晚上这一日三餐都是由我妈妈负责管理……然后晚上我又挤时间陪孩子……然后白天的这个伙食有外婆管理，它有保障，对，就是有这样的分工。
Case B · The HusbandWhen asked directly, Gong hesitates — then quietly locates all the housework in her own hands, with her husband's abstention explained as skill deficit.
It's mostly me doing the housework… meals, laundry — basically me. My husband… cooking isn't really his thing. Probably because at home it was always his mother who cooked.
Teacher Gong
好像家务活都是我一个人在干……换洗的衣物基本上都是我在负责……像我老公……吃的东西还是我这边做得多一点，因为他不太会烧，就是他可能原来在家都是他妈妈煮。
Care Chain
Grandmothermeals
→
Motherevenings
→
Childlearning

Pressure is displaced along a chain of women — grandmother → mother → daughter. The husband's abstention is naturalised as a skill gap; the grandmother's labour is treated as guaranteed infrastructure. Here, "balance" means identifying who is available to absorb the pressure.

04.3 · Affective Bordering
17 / 25

The Emotional Domain

Praise labour  ·  emotion rules
I have to praise every piece of work… So I rack my brain to find something new to say. By the end I almost have nothing left.
每个作品我都要夸，我不能大部分孩子夸完之后，留下那几个孩子难过吧，所以我就绞尽脑汁给他们编，说到最后我都要没话说了。
Teacher Yu
Praise as sustained linguistic labour.
You can't have negative emotions… Most importantly, it protects yourself.
但是不能有负面的情绪，不能带着情绪去工作……不能带有负面情绪，这样最重要的是保护自己。
Teacher Gong
Emotional control as professional norm and self-protection.
The Cost

Praise labour and emotion suppression carry real costs. They draw on the same finite reserves of mental focus and physical health that teaching itself demands. What is spent on producing warmth is subtracted from lesson preparation, classroom presence, and the teacher's own body.

Affective Bordering

How the Border Is Built

  • ♡
    Praise, encouragement, empathy seen as "natural" patience for women.
  • ✿
    Care becomes professional duty; emotional labour becomes unpaid service.
  • ☘
    Maternal experience is absorbed as resource — and returns as over-diagnosis to one's own child.

Acts of Redrawing

  • ✓
    Refusing exchange of evaluation.
  • ✓
    Selectively responding to parents.
  • ✓
    Stepping back from over-questioning.
  • ✓
    Reframing "love" as work that needs recognition.
Key Insight
These acts leave the structure in place while returning invisible emotional labour to a language in which it can be discussed, measured, and redistributed.

06 · Contributions & Limitations
23 / 25

Contributions

Reframe
The teacher-mother is an institutionally bordered position rather than a private role conflict.
Name
Affective bordering names how patience, praise, and self-restraint are pulled from women teachers as unpaid work.
Compare
A situated Chinese case enters dialogue with Korean and Indonesian feminist scholarship.Park (2007); Adriany (2018)
Redistribute
Responsibility must be redrawn across school, family, and society.

Limits & Future

Sample
Only three teachers in Jiangsu cannot represent regional and structural variation.
Method
The family field relies on self-report, not in-home observation.
Scope
Sino-Korean and Sino-Japanese comparison has not yet been developed.
Next
Affective bordering can extend to other feminised care professions.nursing · ECE · social work
