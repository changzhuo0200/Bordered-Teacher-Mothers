A Feminised Profession Under Pressure

Structural · Cultural · Policy
StructuralReality
~70% women teachers in compulsory education; 74.48% in primary schools (MoE 2023; ~75% in 2024). Underrepresented in leadership and compensation.
CulturalConstraint
The profession is maternalised — "stability", "long vacations" naturalise the gendered division of labour.
PolicyImpetus
Post-Double Reduction: non-teaching tasks ↑ and role conflicts intensified.

Research Questions

One core question  ·  three sub-questions
How are women primary teachers placed at a bordered position among public teaching, private mothering, and social reproduction — and who bears the institutional cost?
Q1 · Institutional

School–Family Borders

How are responsibility borders moved through collaboration, tutoring, parent communication, and evaluation?

Q2 · Reproductive

Time · Body · Kinship

Once pressure enters the family, how is it redistributed along gendered and intergenerational lines?

Q3 · Affective

Naturalised Emotion

How are patience, empathy, and self-restraint converted into unpaid emotional labour?

02 · Research Methodology

Feminist Qualitative Case + Narrative Inquiry

Hesse-Biber (2014); Riessman (2008); Stake (1995)
i.

Literature Review

86 documents — teacher-mother scholarship, emotional labour, educational maternalisation, Asian developmentalist–neoliberal governance.

ii.

Semi-Structured Interviews

3 women primary teachers, Jiangsu; 1–2 interviews each. Daily life, school–family communication, mothering, division of labour, emotional management.

iii.

Participant Observation

Office observation with one participant — school–family communication, fragmented time, colleagues' parenting talk, teaching / mothering interaction.

iv.

Thematic Analysis

Braun & Clarke (2006). Three themes: moving school–family borders, second shift & care chains, maternalised emotional labour. Pseudonyms throughout.

05 · Discussion & Conclusion
14 / 18

Responses to Q1 · Q2 · Q3

Three chapters answering the three sub-questions.
Q1Institutional

Ch.1 · School–Family Borders

School–family collaboration is both cooperation and a conduit through which educational responsibility descends onto teachers' individual time and emotional labour. Teachers are pushed into the roles of developmental judge, parent emotion coordinator, and after-school compensator — silently, and without being named as expansion.

Q2Reproductive

Ch.2 · Time · Body · Kinship

Home is not a private space outside the institution but where social reproduction continues to operate. Pressure is absorbed at three registers — time, body, and division of labour — producing a hidden care infrastructure embedded in the mother–grandmother chain.

Q3Affective

Ch.3 · Naturalised Emotion

Once schools take praise, encouragement, and empathy as women teachers' "natural" patience, professional labour is converted into unpaid emotional service. Teacher-mothers respond through limited, negotiable withdrawals — refusing exchange of evaluation, and reframing "love" as work that requires institutional recognition.

05 · The Core Answer
15 / 18

Redrawing the Border

The teacher-mother's predicament is not private role conflict — it is an institutionally bordered position.
i.Schools

cannot transfer

after-school tutoring, school–family emotional coordination, and family-education compensation onto individual teachers under the discourse of "obligation".

ii.Families

cannot default

childrearing, homework companionship, housework, and emotional support to women — nor treat the grandmother–mother–child chain as invisible infrastructure.

iii.Society & Policy

cannot naturalise

labour value behind the rhetoric of "dedication", "love", and "suitable for women" — patience, praise, and empathy are work that requires institutional recognition.

*Restructuring

begins the moment

naturalised care labour becomes something that can be discussed, named, and redistributed.

06 · Contributions & Limitations
16 / 18

Contributions

Reframe
The teacher-mother is an institutionally bordered position, not a private role conflict.
Name
Affective bordering names how patience, praise, and self-restraint are pulled from women teachers as unpaid work.
Compare
A situated Chinese case enters dialogue with Korean and Indonesian feminist scholarship.Park (2007); Adriany (2018)
Redistribute
Responsibility must be redrawn across school, family, and society.

Limits & Future

Sample
Only three teachers in Jiangsu cannot represent regional and structural variation.
Method
The family field relies on self-report, not in-home observation.
Scope
Sino-Korean and Sino-Japanese comparison has not yet been developed.
Next
Affective bordering can extend to other feminised care professions.nursing · ECE · social work
